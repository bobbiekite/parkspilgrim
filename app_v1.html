Secular meaning-making, in our own backyard.

A pilgrimage doesn't require a far-off shrine. It asks only that you arrive somewhere with intention, and let the place do its work.

What is secular pilgrimage?

Old practice, no doctrine required.

For thousands of years, people have set out toward meaningful places on purpose: to mark a passage, to grieve, to give thanks, to understand where they came from.

Secular pilgrimage keeps the practice and sets aside the requirement of religion. You bring your own questions. The journey is structured but unhurried: a place chosen with care, time set apart, attention paid. What you make of it is yours.

It works because the impulse was never really about the destination. It was about going differently: slowly, deliberately, and open to being changed by what you find.

You don't have to travel far. You have to travel with intention.

Four pilgrimage pathways

Where a pilgrimage can take you

There's no single right place. These four pathways are simply doorways. Pick the one that calls, or move between them over a lifetime.

i.

National Park Service Sites

The full 433: not only the 63 marquee parks but the battlefields, seashores, monuments, and historic homes that hold the country's nature, heritage, and civic memory.

ii.

State, Regional & Local Sites

Courthouses, county museums, state parks, the overlook ten minutes from home. The places you already pass, approached this time on purpose.

iii.

CCC & New Deal Legacy Sites

Where land, history, and public service converge: the trails, lodges, and stonework built by a generation that believed the commons was worth shaping by hand.

iv.

Your Own Sacred Place

Anywhere that already holds meaning for you: a river, a grave, a kitchen table. You don't have to go far. You just have to go differently.

Three kinds of journey

What you're really going toward

Every site speaks in more than one register. Most pilgrimages lean into one of these three.

Nature

Canyon, prairie, old-growth, fossil bed. The pilgrimage of scale and deep time, where standing in the right place quietly rearranges your sense of your own.

Heritage

The places where memory is kept, including the hard ones. Going to bear witness, to connect a lineage, to let a story be more than a paragraph in a book.

Civic

Civil War battlefields, civil rights landmarks, courthouses and capitols, the sites of public reckoning. The pilgrimage of belonging: to a community, a country, an ongoing and unfinished experiment.

About the founder

Bobbie Kite

Founder · PhD · MTS · Lifelong pilgrim

American Parks Pilgrimage grew out of one person's practice. Over 350 documented visits across all 63 national parks and 250 of the official 433 NPS sites, not as a checklist but as a way of paying attention.

With a PhD and a Master of Theological Studies, Bobbie brings together the scholar's eye and the pilgrim's heart: rigorous about history and place, unhurried about meaning. The work now is sharing the practice: guiding others to approach the sites around them with the same intention.
